Croatian Pronunciation Guide for English Speakers

Croatian pronunciation differs from English in several key ways: (1) spelling is phonetic—each letter usually has a single sound, (2) all vowels are clear and pure (no diphthongs as in English), (3) the letter r is rolled/tapped, not the English [ɹ], (4) Croatian has several palatal consonants (č, ć, dž, đ, lj, nj, š, ž), and (5) stress can fall on different syllables but is not marked in writing.

Croatian Sound / Feature IPA Example (Croatian) Closest English Cue
Pure vowels a, e, i, o, u mama [ˈma.ma], vino [ˈʋi.no] a = “father”, e = “met”, i = “machine”, o = “more” (rounded), u = “flute”. Never glide into diphthongs.
Rolled r r ruka [ˈru.ka] (“hand”) Rolled/trilled like in Spanish “perro.” Never English [ɹ].
č vs. ć tʃ ~ tɕ čaj [tʃaj] (“tea”) vs. ćao [tɕa.o] (“hi”) č = “ch” in church. ć is softer, like Polish ć or “t” in “nature” (fast speech).
đ vs. dž dʑ ~ dʒ đak [dʑak] (“pupil”) vs. džem [dʒem] (“jam”) dž = “j” in jam. đ is softer, closer to “dy” in “duke” (British).
š and ž ʃ ~ ʒ šuma [ˈʃu.ma] (“forest”), žena [ˈʒe.na] (“woman”) š = “sh” in she; ž = “s” in measure.
lj and nj ʎ ~ ɲ mlijeko [mʎiˈɛ.ko] (“milk”), konj [koɲ] (“horse”) lj ≈ Italian “gli” in famiglia; nj ≈ Spanish “ñ” in niño.
Stress ˈ gláva vs. glàva Stress can shift meaning. It’s marked with an accent in dictionaries, not in everyday writing.

Additional Rules

- Croatian spelling is almost one-to-one with sounds: write as you speak, speak as you write.
- The letters q, w, x, y appear only in foreign words.
- Every consonant is pronounced clearly, even at word-end (no silent letters).
- Unstressed vowels stay clear—never reduced to “uh” like English schwa.
- Double consonants (nn, ss, ll, etc.) are held longer.
